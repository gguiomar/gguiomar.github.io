Here's an example with an inline equation: The energy-momentum relation is given by $E = \sqrt{(pc)^2 + (mc^2)^2}$, where $E$ is energy, $p$ is momentum, $m$ is mass, and $c$ is the speed of light.

For displayed equations, use double dollar signs. Here's the Schrödinger equation:

$$ i\hbar\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\Psi(\mathbf{r},t) = \left[-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\nabla^2 + V(\mathbf{r},t)\right]\Psi(\mathbf{r},t) $$
